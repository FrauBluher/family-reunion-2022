December 2025

Dear friends and family,

Looks like 2025 is in the (slightly askew and damaged by Vito's hand) rear-view mirror. Here's a small view into our largely Vito-themed 2025. Click on the images to enlarge.

We started our 2025 with a teeny baby Vito. We were able to sprinkle in some standard winter fare with the support of friends and family, though spent the majority of our time enjoying Vito's newfound joys.

In retrospect the passing of 2025 in our memory is not marked primarily by the changing of seasons, but by Vito's growth from a baby to a toddler. The year has progressed along the trend from barely mobile through to bounding away from us at surprising speeds. From easily trapped to an ungovernable being whose curiosity cannot be contained.

We travelled this past year with intrepid little Vito in tow.

Among the reasons for our travels were the multiple weddings we attended together!

We were also fortunate to take two trips to California to visit friends and enjoy the beautiful scenery and some early foliage (from the east-coast perspective).

Brass Tack lived to see another season of adventures on Lake Cayuga with a new pilot at the helm.

A new project and home-away for the grandparents took shape in the form of a cozy yurt in Ithaca just outside of town.

A major home improvement project was our kitchen renovation in Ithaca. Bringing to us a fresh and functional space to cook and gather - and most importantly the first dishwasher of our relationship of 10 years(!).

We were fortunate to enjoy time with our far-flung loved ones and Vito's first summer retreat to Cape Cod.

Warmer days brought us to familiar spots around Ithaca including the farmers market, as well as time in the garden. Vito and Pavlo also visited the Cornell baby lab to participate in a study on infant development.

As the leaves turned so too did Vito's demeanor. His one-day punk phase shocked us all.

Pavlo's work adventures were generally muted this year with Vito's presence as an anchor to Ithaca, though he did have one short trip to Alaska (and one analogous work event in upstate NY). Sarah's professional life brought the family to lovely Banff, and her summer teaching program had Vito filling weather balloons with hydrogen.

Despite the year ending with Vito in his own apartment and chores, there are still some continued truths to count on: boxes are fun, Cheerios are delicious, and winter holidays are a time for family.

We wish a happy new year to you and yours! We close out out letter to you with Vito's Column.
