December 2025

Dear friends and family,

Looks like 2025 is in the rear-view mirror (slightly askew and smudged with Vito's hand print). Here's a small view into our 2025. Click on the images to enlarge.

We started 2025 with a little baby. We were able to sprinkle in some standard winter fare with the support of friends and family, though spent the majority of our time enjoying Vito's newfound joys.

In our memory, the passing of 2025 is not marked primarily by the changing of seasons, but by Vito's growth from a baby to a toddler. From barely mobile to bounding away from us at surprising speeds. From easily trapped to an ungovernable being whose curiosity cannot be contained.

We made many trips this year with intrepid little Vito in tow.

From a backyard in Ithaca to a resort in Cabo San Lucas and venues in California and Montana, weddings were the source of much of our travel.

We were also fortunate to take two trips to California to visit friends and family, where we enjoyed the beautiful verdant scenery and warm weather.

Brass Tack lived to see another season of adventures on Cayuga Lake with a new pilot at the helm.

A cozy yurt in Ithaca just outside of town became a new project and home-away for the grandparents.

Another major project was our kitchen renovation. Bringing to us a fresh and functional space to cook and gather---and most importantly the first dishwasher of our relationship of 10 years(!).

We were fortunate to enjoy time with our far-flung loved ones and Vito's first Cape Cod vacation.

Our warm weather day-to-day in Ithaca includes the farmers market, coffee shops, and our garden. Vito and Pavlo also visited the Cornell Baby Lab to participate in a study on infant development.

As the leaves turned so too did Vito's demeanor. His one-day punk phase shocked us all.

Pavlo's work developing radios brought him to remote test locations including Alaska and Sarah's parents' backyard. Highlights from Sarah's professional life included a weather balloon launch for a summer outreach program and a trip for the family to Banff.

Vito has now moved into his own apartment and contributes diligently to household chores---they grow up so fast! There are still some continued truths to count on: boxes are fun, Cheerios are delicious, and winter holidays are a time for family and snow.

We close out our letter to you with Vito's Column.
