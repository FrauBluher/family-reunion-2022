December 2023

Dear friends and family,

With the passing of 2023, our time in Ithaca now nears two and a half years; we're thankful to have many ways to live well in our new home. In this brief retrospectus, please allow us to sample from our past year and share a bit of what 2023 brought to us.

Early winter in Ithaca presents sporadic snow. In turn, it is becoming a yearly tradition to enjoy cross country skiing on barely passable trails. Dodging rocks, mud, and sticks while zipping over ice - special training for later in the season!

The passing of time in our new home is punctuated by seasonal shifts in the things that live around us. Squirrels and chipmunks fatten for the cold climate, salamanders migrate yearly in fantastic numbers across a few nights at the first turn of spring, water falls as precipitation mounts, and folks parade to mark the peak of temperate weather.

Our new home in the east has a counterpart in the west, one we are fortunate to be able to visit often.

Among the new additions to our routine in Ithaca is dinghy sailing on a Yupa. Lake Cayuga's has provided a great range of conditions for Sarah to teach Pavlo how to sail. A small slip for the boat within walking distance of our home proved to be convenient, though necessitated the installation of a small trolling motor to allow us to get out from the inlet into the lake itself (a storied journey). This year we've motored, sailed, hand cranked, and even resorted to paddling Yupa to her dock.

Our small patio pad installation has supported countless summer nights of grilled meals with friends and family. The warm weather also brought together a multi-hull sailing group which ultimately let us learn how to sail 14 and 16 foot Hobie catamarans. On the Great Sacandaga Lake, our clan met to enjoy time aboard Black Pearl where everyone took the helm on a partifularly swift day. In cape cod we turned to water transportation and leisure over the waves by means of double kayak.

While we have become comfortable in the waters of Lake Cayuga, we spent much of the boating season building confidence that we wouldn't flip Yupa - Once we proved to ourselves that we could team Yupa well, we looked forward to a day with strong winds, waves to thrill and test our skills. With our wetsuits donned and the threat of dropping the mast into the water present, we pressed into the perfect day only to have the mast itself drop due to a cotter pin that had come loose (and barely held on for at least two full sailing sessions!). All damage from this event was repaired and we were able to subsequently sail again before the season ended.

We are thankful to have had the opportunity to share in the union of 3 close couples, all in our respective home towns!

Our home has also changed significantly this year with: The small repair of bespoke cedar shake on the treasure tree in our driveway, the successful resurgance of our planterbox rhubarb and vegetable gardening, and the welcome addition of a new roof (shingles / underlayment / deck).

Halloween in 2023 was shared with friends and family as our record of 303 trick-or-treaters was broken by a healthy 321.

The winter holidays brought us three thanksgivings (Canadian - top row of photos, Friendsgiving, Thanksgiving), and a festive / relaxing December, punctuated by Christmas.

Looking forward to sharing time you in the coming '24.

Happy new year to you and yours!
Sarah and Pavlo
