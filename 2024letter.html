December 2024

Dear friends and family,

There goes 2024! Allow us to share some moments with you from this past year. Click the photos to enlarge.

We started our 2024 with travel to California and NYC. We sampled regional cuisines, from sushi to pizza, and had our fill of Silcon Valley startups. We also snuck in some XC skiing despite a relatively snowless winter.
In spring we scheduled a total solar eclipse for Sarah's birthday, refurbished a 1972 Tanzer 22 sailboat, and successfully christened Brass Tack for a season of sailing.

Spring brought favorable terrestrial weather and unfavorable solar weather — the perfect conditions for home improvement and the start of new projects. Not pictured: wasp extraction from the treasure tree using a shop vac full of soapy water.

This year's Ithaca delights were punctuated by a visit from the Oscar Mayer Wienermobile, the reintroduction of the annual rubber duck race, a new furry friend, cargo-bike trips to the farmer's market, and our first Porchfest (after missing it for three years in a row)!

2024's distinguished events included the graduation of Sarah's first PhD student, and three wonderful weddings in Philadelphia, Queensbury, and Ocean City.

Between Pavlo's new Swiss job and Sarah's conference in Vienna, we were able to sneak in a European vacation before our yearly summer retreat to Cape Cod. Some highlights included a river tube through Zurich and a hike on Stoos. On the drive home from the Cape, our car turned 100k.

We were lucky enough to have Brass Tack survive the active sailing season with few crises and plenty of memorable moments. Our favorite was the rescue of an intrepid Sunfish sailor whose mast came down during an especially windy day on Cayuga Lake.

If you hadn't noticed from the photos above, 2024 was also the year of Sarah's pregnancy and the arrival of our wonderful baby boy, Vito! Victor Oleander Denovi was born on October 10th, 2024 into a welcoming community of loved ones. We are overjoyed and thankful beyond words. :)

Our day-to-day is now defined by our little guy and rapid growth, both as baby and parents.

Vito arrived in time to activate "Plan A" for Halloween. ("Plan B" involved an orange painted stomach.) This year we declared defeat after running out of candy at 7:30 PM to the hands of ~250 trick-or-treaters.

Vito had the honor of being visited by distinguished elders. Our first couple months with Vito were made infinitely easier with the love and support of family.

We celebrated a Thanksgiving with many thanks to give. Vito continues to become more active, alert, and vocal.

And finally, we enjoyed a white Christmas and the cold and snowy close to 2024.

With joy in our hearts, and ringing in our ears, we wish a happy new year to you and yours!
Sarah and Pavlo
